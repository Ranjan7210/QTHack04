What Happens Next?

An interactive timeline of quantum computing's trajectory.

2024–2025

NISQ Era: Noisy Intermediate Scale

50–1000 qubit processors with error rates ~0.1–1%. Useful for quantum chemistry, small optimizations, and proof-of-concept demonstrations. Error correction is experimental.

2027–2030

Fault-Tolerant Threshold

First logical qubits with error correction. Drug discovery simulations become practical. Post-quantum cryptography becomes mandatory for banking and government.

2030–2035

Quantum Advantage at Scale

1,000+ logical qubits. Material science revolution — room-temperature superconductors designed. AI training accelerated from weeks to hours. Climate modeling breakthroughs.

2035–2040+

Quantum-Native Computing

Quantum processors in data centers alongside classical systems. Fully homomorphic encryption. Quantum internet for unhackable communication. A new era of computing begins.

🔓

Cybersecurity Apocalypse?

+

Shor's algorithm can break RSA, ECC, and Diffie-Hellman — the backbone of internet security. Every encrypted message ever sent could be retroactively decrypted ("harvest now, decrypt later" attacks).

The fix: Post-quantum cryptography (lattice-based, hash-based) is being standardized by NIST right now. Migration is urgent.

💊

Medicine Revolution

+

Molecular simulation at quantum scale means designing drugs atom by atom. Protein folding becomes exact, not approximate. Personalized medicine based on full molecular profiles becomes feasible.

Impact: Drug development time drops from 10–15 years to months. Diseases like Alzheimer's become treatable.

🤖

AI Singularity Accelerated

+

Quantum machine learning can process exponentially larger feature spaces. Training that takes weeks on GPU clusters could take hours on quantum processors.

Risk: AI development accelerates beyond our ability to control. Quantum-enhanced AI could design even better quantum computers, creating a feedback loop.

🌍

Global Technology Shift

+

Quantum computing becomes a strategic national asset. Countries without quantum capabilities face technological dependency. New "quantum divides" emerge between nations.

Opportunity: Climate change modelling becomes precise enough to predict and prevent catastrophic events. Energy grid optimization saves billions.
